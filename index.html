Manosaviya

Year 1

Module: GTEC 13032 - Projects in Technology - I

Objective: To provide university students with a safe and anonymous platform to support their mental health. Because most of the students have a fear of judgment about their mental health conditions. Through this Manosaviya website, connect the students with trusted mentors, those who can support emotional support and guidance.

Tools: Figma, Wordpress, Google Forms

Full Description: Manosaviya is a conceptual website and designed to support the mental well-being of university students, those who experience stress, university rag, and loneliness, like emotional things. Research encountered the most of the students suffer with their mental issues, but they lack awareness of mental health services, and they do not like to get help because of fear of judgement.This website focuses on anonymous interaction It allows for students to safely connect with their mentors without sharing their identities. Students can book appointments with mentors and connect with them. Through this website, build a user-friendly platform to connect them. Through this project, my key role is to research student mental health and their ideas about this method and make online surveys and gather information and others.

Outcomes: Identify the mental health challenges faced by university students and find a solution to their problem and design an anonymous safety platform for this.

Based Line Follower Robot

Year 2

Module: GTEC 21023 - Fundamentals of Electronics

Objective: To design and prototype a line-following robot's electronic components and gain experience in electronic circuit design, PCB design, testing, and debugging. And also improve knowledge about the electronic components and how they are used and gain experience. To enhance teamwork and faced the challenges gained experinces.

Full Description: We build a transistor-based line-following robot. When the robot follows a black line on a white background, if it detects an obstacle, it stopped. These robots are not allowed to have motor controller modules, microcontrollers, or programmable devices. This is made by using transistors or nonprogrammable ICs. The robot uses IR sensors to detect the difference between the black line and the white surface area. Designing the PCB board and designing the circuits through guidance and building the circuit are key roles of our project.

Outcomes: Final outcome is a demonstrated fully functional analog robot, and gained knowledge about how to work with and use transistors and other components and the system. Enhancing strengthening of teamwork, communication and time-management.
